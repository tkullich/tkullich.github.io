Tyler K's Background



Highschool Years and Associate Degree

I will be honest and tell you that I never imagined myself graduating from a university. I was careless and that was reflected in my 2.4 GPA. Neither of my parents graduated Highschool and there was no pressure for me to differentiate myself from them. I saw the struggle they've had with that decision and I decided for myself that I wanted something far greater. I chose to start my higher education by attending Mercer County Community College back in 2013. At first, I had started on a career path in Criminal Justice. I soon realized that I wanted to pursue a much more technical career in Information Technology. I not only changed my area of study to Computer Information Systems (CIS), but I also changed my entire mindset to pursue a career in technology. As my mindset changed, my attention to schoolwork and assignments became prioritized. I dug myself out of my poor study habits and developed a goal-oriented mindset. I finished the final semester of my A.S. with a term GPA of 3.89 and cumulative GPA of 3.4.

Undergraduate Degree

After I have received my A.A. I planned on finding an entry level position while working to obtain various IT certificates (compTIA A+, Network+, Security+, ect). After job hunting for a while with no results, my mindset shifted once again. With the support from my then-girlfriend, now wife, I had decided to pursue my bachelor's degree. I applied to one University at first, Rider University. Not only was I was accepted, but I was also given a scholarship that covered half of the tuition. After meeting with one of the advisors, I was given a visual print of the tuition costs and fees after my scholarship. Because Rider is a private university, I would receive close to nothing from state and federal financial aid. The tuition and fee costs were far too high for me to accept and I decided to decline their offer. I soon after applied to Rutgers University New Brunswick and heard back a few months later. I was accepted to the School of Arts and Sciences. After seeing the tuition and fee costs, I knew Rutgers was a much better choice for me personally. I had done my research and concluded that I wanted to take part in their Information Technology and Informatics program. After my first semester, I was able to apply for entry into the program. I was also prompted to select a minor area of study, which for that, I choose Human Resource Management. As I write this in my room, I am in my final semester at Rutgers and in my program. I am proud to say that I have consecutively been on the Dean's List every semester I have been attending. I am also delighted to state that I currently hold a 3.9 GPA and anticipate graduating this spring 21'.
